How to Set Up a Chess Board

  • Sep 24, 2023

Welcome to Jorrod.com, where we make learning chess easy and enjoyable. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the step-by-step process of setting up a chessboard so you can start your chess journey on the right foot. Follow the steps below to set up your board correctly like the image below.

Step 1: Get Your Chess Board Ready

Before you begin, make sure you have a standard 8x8 chessboard with alternating light and dark squares. Configure the board so that the bottom right square is a light-colored square for both players.

Step 2: Familiarize Yourself with the Chess Pieces

In chess, there are six different types of pieces, each with its unique movement and value:

  • King: The most important piece in the game. In most sets, it will often be the tallest piece with a cross over his head.
  • Queen: The most powerful piece, often referred to as the “Queen of the board.”
  • Rook: Castle-like pieces that move in straight lines.
  • Knight: Distinctive horse-shaped pieces that move in an L-shape.
  • Bishop: Pieces that move diagonally across the board.
  • Pawn: The smallest but essential pieces on the board.

Step 3: Placing the Pieces

Now, let’s set up the board. For this guide, we’ll assume you’re playing as White.

White’s Side:

  • Rooks: Place the two white Rooks in the corners of the board (a1 and h1).
  • Knights: Next to the Rooks, position the two white Knights (b1 and g1).
  • Bishops: Place the white Bishops next to the Knights (c1 and f1).
  • Queen: The white Queen goes on the central white square of the first row (d1).
  • King: Finally, put the white King beside the Queen (e1).
  • Pawns: Line up all eight white Pawns on the second row (a2 through h2).

Black’s Side:

Now, mirror the setup for the black pieces on the opposite side of the board:

  • Rooks: Black Rooks in the corners (a8 and h8).
  • Knights: Black Knights next to the Rooks (b8 and g8).
  • Bishops: Place the black Bishops next to the Knights (c8 and f8).
  • Queen: The black Queen on the central dark square of the last row (d8).
  • King: Finally, put the black King beside the Queen (e8).
  • Pawns: Line up all eight black Pawns on the seventh row (a7 through h7).

Double-Check Your Setup

Before you start playing, double-check the following:

All the pieces are correctly placed on their respective squares. The board is oriented correctly, with a white square on your right side.

With your chessboard set up correctly, you’re now ready to dive into the exciting world of chess. If you’re new to the game, check out our beginner’s guide to understanding the rules and basic strategies.

Enjoy your games, practice, and explore more chess resources on Jorrod.com. Happy playing!
